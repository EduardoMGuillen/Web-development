The Criminal Defense Process

Understanding your rights and the legal process when facing criminal charges

1

Arrest & Initial Contact

Immediate legal representation from the moment of arrest, ensuring your rights are protected and proper procedures are followed.

Immediate

Critical Actions:

  • Remain silent
  • Request attorney
  • Document circumstances
  • Contact family
2

Bail Hearing & Release

Advocating for your release on reasonable bail terms while preparing for the upcoming legal proceedings.

24-48 hours

Required Documents:

  • Bail application
  • Financial information
  • Character references
  • Employment verification
3

Arraignment & Plea

Formal reading of charges and entering of initial plea, with strategic guidance on plea options and next steps.

1-2 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Court appearance
  • Charge review
  • Plea strategy
  • Discovery request
4

Discovery & Investigation

Thorough investigation of evidence, witness statements, and legal precedents to build the strongest possible defense.

2-8 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Evidence review
  • Witness interviews
  • Expert consultations
  • Legal research
5

Pre-Trial Motions

Filing strategic motions to suppress evidence, dismiss charges, or resolve issues before trial begins.

2-6 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Motion to suppress
  • Motion to dismiss
  • Evidence challenges
  • Legal briefs
6

Trial or Resolution

Aggressive trial representation or strategic plea negotiations to achieve the best possible outcome for your case.

1-4 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Trial preparation
  • Jury selection
  • Witness testimony
  • Closing arguments

Criminal Defense Process Overview

The complete criminal defense process typically takes 3-12 months depending on the complexity of the case, court schedules, and whether the case goes to trial. Our experienced team ensures aggressive representation at every stage while protecting your constitutional rights.
