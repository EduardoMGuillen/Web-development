Criminal Defense Process

Your step-by-step guide through the criminal justice system

1

Arrest & Initial Contact

The moment you're arrested or under investigation, contact us immediately. Time is critical in criminal cases, and early intervention can make a significant difference in the outcome.

Within 24 hours
  • Emergency legal consultation
  • Bail hearing preparation
  • Initial case assessment
  • Rights protection
2

Investigation & Evidence Review

We conduct a thorough investigation of your case, reviewing all evidence, police reports, and witness statements to identify weaknesses in the prosecution's case.

1-2 weeks
  • Evidence collection and analysis
  • Witness interviews
  • Police report review
  • Expert consultation
3

Defense Strategy Development

Based on our investigation, we develop a comprehensive defense strategy tailored to your specific case, focusing on the strongest possible defense.

2-3 weeks
  • Legal research and precedent analysis
  • Defense theory development
  • Motion preparation
  • Strategy refinement
4

Pre-Trial Motions

We file strategic pre-trial motions to suppress evidence, dismiss charges, or limit the prosecution's case, often leading to reduced charges or case dismissal.

2-4 weeks
  • Motion to suppress evidence
  • Motion to dismiss charges
  • Discovery motions
  • Constitutional challenges
5

Plea Negotiations

We negotiate with prosecutors to secure the best possible plea agreement, which may include reduced charges, lighter sentences, or alternative sentencing options.

2-6 weeks
  • Prosecutor negotiations
  • Charge reduction discussions
  • Sentencing alternatives
  • Plea agreement review
6

Trial Preparation

If a favorable plea cannot be reached, we prepare for trial by organizing evidence, preparing witnesses, and developing compelling arguments for your defense.

4-8 weeks
  • Witness preparation
  • Evidence organization
  • Jury selection strategy
  • Opening/closing arguments
7

Trial & Resolution

We aggressively represent you at trial, presenting your defense to the jury and fighting for an acquittal or the best possible outcome.

1-2 weeks
  • Jury trial representation
  • Cross-examination of witnesses
  • Evidence presentation
  • Verdict and sentencing
