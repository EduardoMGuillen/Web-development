Heirship Proceedings

Establish Legal Ownership of Inherited Property

What are Heirship Proceedings?

Heirship proceedings are legal processes used to establish the rightful heirs of a deceased person when no will exists or when property was not properly transferred through probate. This court process determines who has legal ownership rights to inherited property and assets.

The Heirship Process

1

File Petition

An interested party files a petition with the court requesting determination of heirship. This includes providing information about the deceased, their family members, and the property in question.

2

Court Investigation

The court appoints an attorney ad litem to investigate the family history, locate potential heirs, and verify the information provided in the petition.

3

Notice to Heirs

All potential heirs must be notified of the proceedings. This includes publishing notice in local newspapers and serving citation to known family members.

4

Court Hearing

A hearing is held where evidence is presented to establish the family tree, verify relationships, and determine the rightful heirs according to Texas law.

5

Judgment

The court issues a judgment declaring the heirs and their respective ownership interests in the deceased person's property.

6

Property Transfer

With the court's judgment, heirs can properly transfer title to property, sell assets, or establish clear ownership for future transactions.
