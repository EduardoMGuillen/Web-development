Advance Directives

Ensure Your Healthcare Wishes Are Honored

What are Advance Directives?

Advance directives are legal documents that communicate your healthcare preferences and treatment wishes when you're unable to speak for yourself. These essential estate planning documents ensure your medical care aligns with your values and beliefs during critical health situations.

Types of Advance Directives

📋

Living Will

Specifies your preferences for end-of-life medical treatment, including life-sustaining measures, artificial nutrition, and pain management when you're terminally ill or in a persistent vegetative state.

Covers:
  • Life support preferences
  • Artificial nutrition and hydration
  • Pain management wishes
  • Resuscitation decisions
👨‍⚕️

Medical Power of Attorney

Designates a trusted person to make healthcare decisions on your behalf when you're unable to communicate your wishes or make medical decisions yourself.

Authorizes:
  • Treatment decisions
  • Hospital and facility choices
  • Medical record access
  • Healthcare provider selection
🔒

HIPAA Authorization

Allows designated individuals to access your protected health information and communicate with your healthcare providers about your medical condition and treatment.

Permits:
  • Medical record access
  • Provider communication
  • Treatment information sharing
  • Insurance coordination
🚫

Do Not Resuscitate (DNR)

A medical order that instructs healthcare providers not to perform CPR or other life-sustaining measures if your heart stops or you stop breathing.

Specifies:
  • No CPR attempts
  • No electrical shock therapy
  • No artificial ventilation
  • Comfort care focus
📄

POLST Form

Physician Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment - a medical order form that translates your treatment preferences into specific medical orders for emergency responders and healthcare providers.

Includes:
  • CPR preferences
  • Medical intervention levels
  • Antibiotic use decisions
  • Feeding tube preferences
🧠

Mental Health Directive

Provides instructions for mental health treatment preferences and designates someone to make mental health decisions during periods of mental incapacity.

Addresses:
  • Psychiatric medication preferences
  • Treatment facility choices
  • Therapy preferences
  • Mental health agent designation

🏥

Give doctors and medical staff clear instructions about your treatment preferences and care goals.

⚖️

Legal Protection

Provide legal authority for your healthcare decisions and protect against unwanted medical interventions.

💰

Creating Your Advance Directives

1

Consultation & Education

Meet with an experienced attorney to understand your options and learn about different types of advance directives available in Texas.

2

Personal Reflection

Consider your values, beliefs, and preferences regarding medical treatment, end-of-life care, and quality of life priorities.

3

Choose Healthcare Agent

Select a trusted individual to serve as your healthcare power of attorney and discuss your wishes with them in detail.

4

Document Preparation

Your attorney prepares customized advance directive documents that reflect your specific healthcare preferences and legal requirements.

5

Execution & Witnesses

Sign the documents in the presence of required witnesses and notary public to ensure legal validity and enforceability.

6

Distribution & Updates

Provide copies to your healthcare agent, family members, doctors, and hospital. Review and update regularly as needed.

Important Considerations

👥

Family Discussions

Have open conversations with family members about your healthcare preferences to ensure everyone understands your wishes and values.

🔄

Regular Reviews

Review and update your advance directives regularly, especially after major health changes, life events, or changes in your values.

📍

Accessibility

Ensure your advance directives are easily accessible to healthcare providers, family members, and your designated healthcare agent.

⚖️

Legal Compliance

Work with an experienced attorney to ensure your documents meet all Texas legal requirements and will be honored by healthcare providers.

🩺

Medical Team Communication

Discuss your advance directives with your primary care physician and specialists to ensure they understand your preferences.

📱

Emergency Access

Consider keeping copies in multiple accessible locations and informing emergency contacts about their existence and location.
