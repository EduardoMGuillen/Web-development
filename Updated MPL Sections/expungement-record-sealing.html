Understanding Record Clearing Options

Expungement and record sealing are legal processes that can help clear your criminal record, giving you a fresh start and new opportunities. In Texas, these procedures can remove or hide certain criminal records from public view, helping you move forward without the burden of past mistakes. Our experienced criminal defense attorneys can help you navigate these complex processes and determine your eligibility.

The Record Clearing Process

1

Case Evaluation

Comprehensive review of your criminal history to determine eligibility for expunction, nondisclosure, or other record clearing options.

2

Record Research

Thorough investigation of all court records, arrest records, and related documentation to ensure complete and accurate petitions.

3

Petition Preparation

Drafting and filing the appropriate legal documents with the court, including all required supporting evidence and documentation.

4

Notice to Agencies

Serving notice to all relevant law enforcement agencies, prosecutors, and other parties as required by law.

5

Court Hearing

Representing you at the court hearing to present evidence and arguments supporting your petition for record clearing.

6

Order Enforcement

Ensuring all agencies comply with the court order and that your records are properly destroyed or sealed as required.
