The Probate Process Explained

Understanding the step-by-step journey through probate administration

1

Initial Consultation & Assessment

We begin with a comprehensive review of the estate, including assets, debts, and family dynamics to determine the best approach.

1-2 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Death certificate
  • Original will (if exists)
  • Asset information
  • Family contact details
2

Filing Probate Petition

We file the necessary legal documents with the probate court to initiate the formal probate process.

2-4 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Probate petition
  • Death certificate
  • Will (if applicable)
  • Filing fees
3

Court Hearing & Appointment

The court holds a hearing to validate the will and appoint an executor or administrator for the estate.

4-8 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Court hearing notice
  • Proof of publication
  • Bond (if required)
  • Letters of administration
4

Asset Inventory & Valuation

We conduct a thorough inventory of all estate assets and obtain professional valuations when necessary.

2-6 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Asset inventory
  • Appraisals
  • Account statements
  • Property records
5

Creditor Notification & Claims

We notify all known creditors and handle any claims against the estate according to legal requirements.

4-12 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Creditor notices
  • Publication of notice
  • Claim forms
  • Payment records
6

Final Distribution & Closing

We distribute the remaining assets to beneficiaries and file the final accounting to close the estate.

2-4 weeks

Required Documents:

  • Final accounting
  • Distribution receipts
  • Closing petition
  • Court order

Probate Process Overview

The complete probate process typically takes 6-18 months depending on the complexity of the estate, court schedules, and any disputes that may arise. Our experienced team ensures efficient handling while maintaining the highest standards of legal compliance.
