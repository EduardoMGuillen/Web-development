The Probate Process

Understanding each step of the probate journey

1

Filing the Petition

The probate process begins with filing a petition with the probate court. We handle all necessary paperwork, including the death certificate, will (if applicable), and initial court filings.

1-2 weeks
  • Petition preparation and filing
  • Death certificate processing
  • Court scheduling
  • Initial notices
2

Appointment of Personal Representative

The court will appoint a personal representative (executor) to manage the estate. We guide you through the appointment process and ensure all legal requirements are met.

2-3 weeks
  • Court hearing attendance
  • Letters of administration
  • Bond arrangements
  • Authority establishment
3

Asset Inventory & Valuation

We assist in identifying, locating, and valuing all estate assets. This includes real estate, bank accounts, investments, personal property, and any other assets owned by the deceased.

4-6 weeks
  • Asset identification
  • Professional appraisals
  • Inventory preparation
  • Court filing
4

Creditor Notification

All known creditors must be notified of the probate proceedings. We publish required notices and handle creditor claims to ensure proper debt settlement.

4-6 months
  • Creditor notifications
  • Public notice publication
  • Claims review
  • Debt settlement
5

Tax Matters

We handle all tax-related matters, including final income tax returns, estate tax returns (if applicable), and ensure all tax obligations are properly addressed.

2-4 months
  • Final tax returns
  • Estate tax planning
  • Tax clearances
  • IRS correspondence
6

Asset Distribution

Once all debts are paid and legal requirements satisfied, we oversee the distribution of remaining assets to beneficiaries according to the will or state law.

2-4 weeks
  • Distribution planning
  • Asset transfers
  • Beneficiary communications
  • Final accounting
7

Estate Closure

The final step involves closing the estate by filing all required documents with the court and ensuring all legal obligations have been fulfilled.

1-2 weeks
  • Final court filings
  • Closure documentation
  • Record maintenance
  • Case completion
